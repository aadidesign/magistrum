# RESEARCH — AUDIENCE PERSONAS

## Persona 1 — Rashid the SME Founder/Owner (PRIMARY)

**Demographic**
- Age range: 38–55
- Life stage: Established business owner (5–20 years in trade)
- Locality: Dubai urban metropolitan, often lives Mirdif / JLT / Business Bay; office in Business Bay, Deira, Al Quoz, or DIP itself
- Income bracket: AED 35K–150K/month personal; running a business doing AED 5M–80M annual revenue
- Nationality mix: Indian/Pakistani/UAE national/Lebanese — the broad "GCC SME" mix
- Primary device: **Mobile-first (iPhone 14+ or Samsung Galaxy)** for browsing, **desktop for actual buying/forms**. Site must be flawless on iPhone 390px.
- Digital literacy: Mid — can navigate SaaS but doesn't enjoy filling 10-field forms. Prefers WhatsApp to email.
- Language: English (workplace and personal). Comfortable reading Arabic but English is the default for B2B.

**Search trigger**
- Existing system pain: "I'm using Tally / QuickBooks / Excel and the FTA Corporate Tax filing is killing me"
- New regulation: "We just registered for Corporate Tax — what software handles this?"
- Growth pain: "We've grown past Excel — need invoicing + inventory + CRM connected"
- Referral: "My CA recommended Zoho Books"

**Top 3 anxieties (specific to this trade in this market)**
1. **"Will my data go to India servers / leave UAE?"** — UAE data sovereignty anxiety is real. Need to address Zoho's UAE data residency clearly.
2. **"How much will this actually cost — software + implementation + the upsells?"** — UAE buyers HATE bait-and-switch. Transparent pricing band (even if not exact) builds trust.
3. **"Will you disappear after implementation?"** — Common bad-experience: consultant disappears post-go-live. Make ongoing support a featured offer, not an afterthought.

**Top 3 trust triggers**
1. **Zoho Authorised Partner badge** (Zoho-issued artwork, not generic) + partner profile URL link
2. **UAE DED trade licence + TRN displayed in footer** — signals real registered FTA entity
3. **Real client names** (with permission) — even 2–3 named UAE clients beats "500+ clients" generic claim. Local SME founders judge by who they recognise.

**What competitors fail to address for this persona**
- They market features ("Zoho Books has bank reconciliation!") — Rashid wants outcomes ("Close month-end 3 days faster")
- They ignore Corporate Tax specifics — Rashid is anxious about FTA penalties
- They don't speak to the cross-border owner (Rashid often has a back office in India/Pakistan and benefits from Magistrum's dual-entity story)

**Funnel preference**
- Primary: **WhatsApp** with a pre-filled message ("Hi — I run a [X] business, interested in [service]")
- Secondary: Phone call (after WhatsApp confirms a slot)
- Email: Only for formal proposals, not for first-touch
- Form: 4 fields max for initial enquiry. Long form = Rashid bounces.

**Copy register**
- Confident but not boastful. UAE B2B audience is suspicious of "world's best" claims.
- British spelling (organisation, optimise, centre, licence)
- Specific numbers > superlatives ("3-day go-live" > "fast implementation")
- AED specifically, not "Dh" or "Dirhams" in formal copy

## Persona 2 — Priya the Finance Manager / CFO (SECONDARY — buying influencer)

**Demographic**
- Age range: 30–48
- Role: Finance Manager, Controller, or fractional CFO at a UAE SME
- Often has CA / ACCA / CIMA qualification
- Locality: same as Rashid (employee in Dubai)
- Income bracket: AED 15K–40K/month
- Primary device: **Desktop during work hours (Mon–Fri 9–6)** — researches solutions while at the office
- Digital literacy: **High** — comfortable with SaaS evaluation, comparison spreadsheets, demo recordings
- Language: English; technical accounting English is native

**Search trigger**
- Tasked by Rashid: "Find us a Zoho partner — I want a shortlist of 3 with prices by Tuesday"
- Year-end migration: planning a Q1 software switch
- VAT / Corporate Tax filing breakdown — needs a tool that handles both

**Top 3 anxieties**
1. **"Can I actually configure VAT 5% + Corporate Tax 9% + Designated Zones correctly?"** — Wants to see implementation includes tax-code configuration, not just generic setup.
2. **"Will Magistrum train MY team or just hand over a config?"** — Knowledge transfer is non-negotiable; she doesn't want to be the only one who knows the new system.
3. **"What about month-end / year-end / audit support?"** — Wants ongoing-support service tiers laid out clearly.

**Top 3 trust triggers**
1. **Detailed methodology** — a clear 4–6 phase implementation breakdown (Discovery → Configure → Migrate → Train → Go-live → Support)
2. **Specific tax certifications / experience** — "Corporate Tax filings supported for [N] clients"
3. **Knowledge transfer artefacts** — "Includes 2-hour team training + SOP document + Loom recordings"

**What competitors fail to address for this persona**
- Most sites talk to the founder, not the finance manager
- Methodology pages are either missing or read like generic agile diagrams
- Training scope is fuzzy — Priya wants hours and deliverables

**Funnel preference**
- Email > WhatsApp (professional context; she wants paper trail)
- Wants downloadable proposal / capability deck
- Will compare 3 partners on a spreadsheet — make our comparison points easy to extract

**Copy register**
- Slightly more technical — comfortable with "GL accounts", "tax codes", "VAT 201", "CT-501"
- Lists and tables > paragraphs
- Wants service-page sub-headings like "What's included", "Timeline", "Deliverables", "Out of scope"

## Persona 3 — Aisha the Aspiring Accountant (TRAINING-SIDE persona)

**Demographic**
- Age range: 22–35
- Life stage: Recent commerce/finance graduate OR experienced accountant upgrading skills
- Locality: Dubai, Sharjah, or Northern Emirates; price-sensitive
- Income/budget: AED 8K–18K/month if employed; personal training budget AED 2K–8K
- Primary device: **Mobile-first** browsing; will use desktop only for the enrolment form
- Digital literacy: Mid–High
- Language: English; some prefer Arabic-language content

**Search trigger**
- "Zoho Books certification Dubai"
- "Tally Prime course UAE"
- "Accounting course with job placement Dubai"
- Job ad mentions "Zoho Books knowledge required"

**Top 3 anxieties**
1. **"Will this actually get me a job?"** — Magistrum's "100% Lifetime Job Assurance" claim from magistrum.in needs to be substantive (placement stats, alumni testimonials)
2. **"Is this a recognised certification or just a paper?"** — Authorised training partner status matters
3. **"How much does it cost and is there a payment plan?"**

**Top 3 trust triggers**
1. **Authorised Zoho Training Institute badge** + Authorised Tally Prime training centre
2. **Alumni success stories** with current employers named
3. **Transparent course fees** + payment plan visibility (rare among competitors)

**Note on this persona for the build**
The directive focuses on the B2B implementation pitch. Training is a separate funnel. I'll include a **dedicated /training landing area** with a link out from the homepage and one or two service pages — sized smaller than the implementation funnel but visible because it is unique to Magistrum vs every other Zoho partner.

## Voice + register summary

| Trait | Setting |
|---|---|
| Tone | Confident, measured, knowledgeable. Avoid hype words. |
| Sentence rhythm | Short. Direct. Mix in one longer sentence per paragraph to keep prose readable. |
| Vocabulary | UAE B2B-native. "TRN", "VAT 201", "Corporate Tax", "FTA", "Mainland vs Free Zone", "Designated Zone". |
| Spelling | British English (UAE convention) |
| Pronouns | "We" for the company; "you" for the reader. Avoid "they" / "our clients" in headlines. |
| Banned phrases | world-class, cutting-edge, passionate, solutions, trusted partner (overused), one-stop shop, leverage, synergy, best-in-class, game-changer, revolutionary, seamless (only if true), turnkey |
| Required phrases | "Zoho Authorised Partner" (verbatim — match Zoho's artwork wording), "Dubai-based", UAE-specific tax terms |
| Currency in copy | "AED 5,500" not "Dh 5,500" or "Dirhams 5,500"; ranges OK |
