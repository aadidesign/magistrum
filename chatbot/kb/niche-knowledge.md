## General trade knowledge

A Zoho Authorised Partner is a Zoho-certified firm that helps businesses implement, customise, train on and support Zoho applications. There are three certification tiers — Authorised, Advanced, Premium — assigned by Zoho based on a partner's value score (clients served, complexity, certifications held, satisfaction).

## How a typical Zoho implementation works

1. Discovery — partner learns the client's business, current systems, gaps
2. Scope and price — written deliverables, timeline, fixed-price quote
3. Configuration — partner sets up the Zoho app(s) in the client's tenant
4. Data migration — historical and master data brought across
5. Training — typically role-based; finance, sales, ops
6. Go-live — cutover from old system to Zoho
7. Post-go-live support — usually 30 days included, retainer optional after

## Common misconceptions

### "Zoho is cheaper, so it must be lower quality."

Zoho's pricing model is volume-based (45+ apps in Zoho One for the price of one Salesforce module). Quality of the individual products is competitive with their pricier alternatives in most categories; depth of features varies by product (Zoho CRM is mature; Zoho Projects is solid but less feature-rich than dedicated PM tools).

### "Zoho is just Indian."

Zoho is headquartered in Chennai but has data centres globally including the Middle East. For UAE clients, data can be stored regionally.

### "Implementation takes months."

For most SMEs, 1-3 weeks is enough. Multi-branch, multi-currency or Zoho One full-stack rollouts can take 6-12 weeks but those are the exception, not the rule.

### "We can self-implement to save money."

Possible for very simple cases. For anything involving UAE VAT, Corporate Tax or migration from another system, partner involvement usually pays for itself in avoided rework and tax errors.

## Trade glossary

- **TRN** — Tax Registration Number, issued by the UAE Federal Tax Authority (FTA) to VAT-registered businesses.
- **VAT 201** — The quarterly VAT return filed in the UAE.
- **CT-501** — Term sometimes used for the UAE Corporate Tax return template; the formal term is "Corporate Tax Return".
- **Designated Zone** — A specific list of Free Zones treated as "outside the UAE" for VAT purposes for goods (not services).
- **Free Zone Person (FZP)** — A business licensed in a UAE Free Zone.
- **Qualifying Income** — Income that qualifies for the 0% Corporate Tax rate for a Qualifying Free Zone Person (QFZP).
- **Small Business Relief** — Election under UAE CT law allowing businesses with revenue below AED 3M to treat taxable income as zero (until the relief sunset date).
- **EmaraTax** — The FTA's online portal for VAT and Corporate Tax submissions.
- **FTA** — Federal Tax Authority, the UAE tax authority.
- **DED** — Department of Economic Development, the Dubai authority issuing mainland trade licences.

## When to deflect to a human

- Specific tax positions or filings — these need a qualified tax adviser, not the chatbot.
- Specific contractual terms (NDAs, MSAs) — escalate to Magistrum directly.
- Anything outside the Zoho/Tally ecosystem.
- Pricing for non-standard scope.
